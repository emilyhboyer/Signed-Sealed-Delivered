1. John Luger. Will

In the name of God Amen. The twentieth day of November in the year
of our Lord God one thousand six hundred sixtie and nine I John Luger-
of Charles County in the province of Maryland being suck of body but of
good and perfect memory praised be god do make and ordain this my
last will and testament in manner and form following

[?] I recommend my soul into the hands of God my Creator [?]
through the [?] of Jesus Christ my redeemer to be made [?]
of life everlasting and I commend my body to the earth where of it tis[?] made
desiring it may be enterned[?] in a Christian manner and for the small Estate
which it hath pleased God to find me and my will and require[?] is it be
dispersed in manner following I will and desire is that my two soons[?]
John Luger and Thomas Luger shall have my manor of one thousand acres
called by Paten St. Barbany Manner and my desire is likewise that my
Daughter Elizabeth Luger shall have three hundred acres joing to the
manner on the North side and my will and bequeath is that my said Daughter
shall have my cow called mealy mouth with all her inrease and I will and
bequeath that my loving wife shall have and enjoy one cow called mully-
with all her increase my will and bequeath is that Thomas Gully and John
Ward be overseers of my Cheloreno[?] Estate my will and bequeath is that my
Dear and Loving wife Martha Luger shall have and enjoy all the rest
of my Estate and I do hereby revoke and null very other forms wills [Page]
Testaments Legacies bequeaths and died of [?] promises or Grants whatsoever
In [?] whereof I have [?] set my hand and seal this the 26th
day of November 1669.

Signed Sealed and Delivered
In the presents of us-
the
Thomas [?] Gully
mark of
George Ledge

At the bottom of the forgoing will was the following [?] to [?]
December the 9th 1669 the above written will by the oaths of Thomas
Gully and George Ledge witness thereunto in solem forme proved
before me
Jn. Blomfield
